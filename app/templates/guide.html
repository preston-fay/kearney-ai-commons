How to Use the AI Skills Library

Learn how to navigate the library, understand the difference between prompts and skills, and get the most out of these AI tools in your consulting work.

Prompts vs Interactive Skills

Prompt

Prompts

Ready-to-use text templates that you copy and paste into any LLM (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.). Perfect for quick insights and one-off analyses.

Best for:

  • •Quick strategic analysis
  • •One-time research queries
  • •Brainstorming sessions
  • •Report drafting assistance
Interactive Skill

Interactive Skills

Advanced tools with code implementations for Claude and templates for other LLMs. Designed for complex, repeatable workflows and data processing.

Best for:

  • •Data analysis and visualization
  • •Automated report generation
  • •Complex multi-step workflows
  • •Repeatable processes

Quick Start Guide

1

Search and Filter

Use the search bar to find prompts and skills by keyword. Filter by type (All, Prompts, Skills) to narrow down results. Browse by category, use case, or audience to find exactly what you need.

2

View Details

Click on any card to see the full content. For prompts, you'll see the complete template. For skills, you'll see tabs for "Claude Code" (Python implementation) and "Any LLM" (universal template).

3

Copy and Customize

Click the "Copy" button to copy the content to your clipboard. Paste it into your preferred LLM or development environment. Customize the template with your specific context, data, and requirements before running.

4

Iterate and Refine

Review the initial results and refine your inputs as needed. Add more context, adjust parameters, or try different variations. The best results often come from iterating on the template based on your specific needs.

Best Practices

  • → Be specific: The more context you provide, the better the results. Include industry details, company specifics, and relevant constraints.
  • → Test multiple LLMs: Different models have different strengths. Try the same prompt in ChatGPT and Claude to compare results.
  • → Start simple: Begin with prompts before moving to interactive skills. Build your confidence and understanding gradually.
  • → Document your modifications: Keep track of how you customize templates. This helps you build a personal library of proven approaches.
  • → Share feedback: Let the team know which prompts and skills work best. Your insights help improve the library for everyone.
